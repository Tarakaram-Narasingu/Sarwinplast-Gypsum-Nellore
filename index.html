Gypsum is a light weight material that we can get in two ways:
1) evaporating sea water in massive prehistoric basins, ie, marine gypsum
2) through mining, ie, mineral gypsum. In terms of its chemical composition, it is Calcium Sulphate Di-hydrate (CaSO4.2H2O) and inherently
contains 21% water by molecular weight.
Gypsum products have been used for centuries in the construction space and are the material of choice because of Gypsums unique properties.
It has excellent insulation properties both thermal and acoustic and can offer very good Passive fire protection, moisture resistance, impact resistance
and vapor control when used in combination with the right systems.
